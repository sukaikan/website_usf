Our Cause

Every day, street animals struggle to survive without shelter, food, or medical attention. Abandoned, injured, and malnourished, they face unimaginable hardships. At Unpad Street Feeding Animal Friend (Peduli Hewan Jalanan Unpad), we believe that every life is precious, and it is our duty to protect and care for these vulnerable creatures.

Our Works

At UnpadSF, we carry out a range of initiatives to help street animals:

To ensure the well-being of campus cats, we provide regular nutritionally balanced meals enriched with vitamins and fish oil. Additionally, we have established feeding stations throughout the campus for their convenience.

To control stray cat overpopulation, we prioritize Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) in densely populated areas of campus cats. Our research using the Lincoln-Peterson index shows TNR is an effective method for managing and reducing the stray cat population.

Our successful reflective collars program increases street animals' visibility during low-light conditions, reducing the risk of accidents in campus' streets thus improving their safety.

We promote education among fellow students and Jatinangor locals through various means such as distributing informative brochures, running digital media campaigns, organizing info sessions, and providing helpful advice. These efforts aim to raise awareness and share knowledge.
