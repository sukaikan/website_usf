Our Cause

Every day, street animals struggle to survive without shelter, food, or medical attention. Abandoned, injured, and malnourished, they face unimaginable hardships. At Unpad Street Feeding Animal Friend (Peduli Hewan Jalanan Unpad), we believe that every life is precious, and it is our duty to protect and care for these vulnerable creatures.

Street Animal Feeding

Street Animal Feeding

To ensure the well-being of campus cats, we provide regular nutritionally balanced meals enriched with vitamins and fish oil. Additionally, we have established feeding stations throughout the campus for their convenience.

Feeding street animals is important because it helps them survive and stay healthy. Stray animals often live in harsh conditions and have to scavenge for food, which can be difficult and dangerous. By feeding them, we can help ensure that they have enough to eat and don't have to resort to eating garbage or other unhealthy things.

In addition to providing food, feeding stray animals can also help build trust between humans and animals. Stray animals are often wary of humans because they have been mistreated or ignored in the past. By feeding them regularly, we can show them that we care about their well-being and are not a threat

Trap-Neuter-Return

To control stray cat overpopulation, we prioritize Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) in densely populated areas of campus cats. Our research using the Lincoln-Peterson index shows TNR is an effective method for managing and reducing the stray cat population.

Trap-neuter-return (TNR) is a method of managing street animal populations by humanely trapping, sterilizing, and returning them to their original locations.

TNR improves the health and welfare of street animals by reducing their stress, aggression, and territorial behavior. It also reduces the spread of diseases and parasites among street animals and humans

Reflective Collar Program

Our successful reflective collars program increases street animals' visibility during low-light conditions, reducing the risk of accidents in the streets thus improving both their and driver's safety.

The project aims to help street animals by providing them with reflective collars that can help prevent accidents and protect them from being relocated.

A reflective collar is a type of collar that has a reflective material on it, which makes it easier to see the animal wearing it in the dark. Reflective collars are useful for street animals because they can help prevent accidents, protect them from being relocated, and show that they are cared for by someone.

Education and Campaign

We promote education among fellow students and Jatinangor locals through various means such as distributing informative brochures, running digital media campaigns, organizing info sessions, and providing helpful advice. These efforts aim to raise awareness and share knowledge.

Animal welfare education is an important component of social change movements sweeping across the world. It helps individuals develop and nurture respect, kindness, empathy, and positive attitudes towards both animals and people. Humane education creates a sense of compassion, justice, and a respect for the value of all living things. It also teaches people how to accept and fulfill their responsibilities to not only companion animals but all forms of life

Rescue & Medical Attention

Sick and injured animals require urgent medical attention. Our volunteers treat various ailments, conduct vaccinations, and spay/neuter programs to reduce their suffering and improve their overall health.
